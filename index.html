Democratic Executive
Committee Manual

2026  ·  Version 1.0  ·  Florida Democratic Party

Florida is a diverse state with large and small counties, urban and rural counties, predominantly Democratic counties, and counties where Democrats are heavily outnumbered. No set of standardized practices can be uniformly applied to such a diverse network.

However, Democratic Executive Committees (DECs) in all counties have common duties and responsibilities as established by Florida Statute, as well as common expectations as established by the Florida Democratic Party (FDP). Each county is a critical part of the state's success.

This manual addresses common duties and expectations and promotes best practices found in Florida's most successful DECs. It addresses requirements established by Florida law and FDP bylaws, as well as operating rules vital to the FDP's continuity and efficiency.

Living Document: This Handbook will be updated and improved as needs change. DEC chairs will make the handbook available to their membership.

Five Important Principles of the DECs

1
It's About the DECs!

None of us is more important than our common objective.

2
Mutual Respect

Maintain a culture of mutual respect, keeping personal bickering to a minimum.

3
Innovation

Smart, creative ideas are the key to success.

4
High Expectations

High expectations of each other, with understanding that everyone is fallible.

5
Openness

Everything we do — meetings, voting, documentation, finances — will be open to all members.

A DEC is an official county arm of the FDP with oversight of all Democratic Party activities within the county. As Tip O'Neill said, "All politics is local."

Responsibilities of the DEC

  1. Effectively support the election of Democrats
  2. Create a 5–10 year strategic plan, updating every 2 years
  3. Create an election plan for every cycle
  4. Advance visibility of the Democratic Party in the county
  5. Register Voters in Democrat-leaning areas regularly
  1. Increase Vote-by-Mail requests for Democrats
  2. Ensure Democratic values are advanced within the county
  3. Engage in local county affairs of interest to Democrats
  4. Communicate with the public regarding elections and candidates

Mandatory DEC Activities

  1. Recruit, train, and retain volunteers
  2. Register Voters
  3. Encourage Vote-by-Mail enrollment
  4. Fundraise for operations and GOTV
  5. Turn out the Democratic base in all elections
  1. Use all voter outreach tools (VAN, texting, social media)
  2. Recruit candidates
  3. Establish a voter protection team
  4. Ensure compliance with statutes and bylaws

Types of DEC Membership

Defined by Florida Statutes, Chapter 103.091 and FDP Bylaws, Article V, Section 1:

1. Elected Members
Chosen in the presidential election-year primary. Serve a four-year term. Precinct or District Committeepersons.
2. Automatic Members
Members by virtue of Florida Statutes — county Democratic elected officials and club/caucus presidents. May vote but not serve as officers.
3. Appointed (At-large) Members
Appointed by Chair, approved by majority vote. Limited to 10% of total elected membership. May vote but not serve as officers.
4. Special At-Large Members
At-large status for a Democratic Party elected official. Not included in the appointed membership quota.

Precinct Systems

Standard Precinct System

Default setup: one man and one woman elected from each precinct. Additional seats added for every 1,000 registered Democrats.

Optional Precinct System

For counties with fewer than 200 precincts. Gives flexibility to add members to cover precincts exceeding 1,000 registered voters.

District System

In counties with more than 100 precincts, a DEC can choose — by majority vote before December 31 in the year before a presidential-election year — to elect members by "election districts" instead of individual precincts.

DEC Members

  • Support precinct/district and county campaign plans
  • Active role in GOTV, Early Voting, and Vote-by-Mail
  • Attend all DEC meetings in person or by proxy
  • Assist in filling precinct/district vacancies
  • Distribute Democratic campaign literature
  • Assist in fundraising activities
  • Take and abide by the Party loyalty oath
  • Serve on one DEC standing committee
  • Participate in Election Day activities
  • Phone banking, canvassing, and rides to the polls

Officers

DEC Chair

Leader and presiding officer of the county arm of the FDP. Responsible for supporting the election of Democrats, executing FDP policies, and maintaining legal and bylaw compliance. Official spokesperson for the Democratic Party within the county.

📄 Full Duties of DEC Chair →
DEC Vice Chair

Elected officer and member of the Steering Committee. Assumes the duties of the Chair in their absence. Serves as ex-officio member of all committees.

DEC Secretary

Keeper of records for the DEC.

  • Take and present minutes at all meetings
  • Forward minutes to Party Affairs within 20 days
  • Send general meeting notice at least 10 days prior
  • Send membership changes to Party Affairs and SOE within 10 days
DEC Treasurer

Chief financial officer for the local party.

  • Deposit all DEC funds in designated bank(s)
  • Keep accurate records of all receipts and expenditures
  • Provide accounting at each DEC meeting
  • File all required reports with the SOE
  • Arrange annual financial audit — filed by April 1

State Committee Members

Facilitate communication between state and local party; take issues from DEC to FDP; report FDP business to DEC; keep DEC informed of all Charter and Bylaw changes; attend all State Executive Committee meetings.

While a quarterly meeting is sufficient for FDP minimum requirements, best practice is a regular monthly meeting, ideally on a consistent date and in a consistent, accessible space.

Methods to Call a Meeting

Call by the Chair

General meetings: at least 10 days' written notice.
Special meetings: at least 5 days' notice; notice must clearly state the purpose.

Call by Petition

Written petition signed by at least 30% of total membership (excluding Automatic members). Chair must schedule within 20 days, with at least 14 days' notice.

Failure-to-Call (Reorganization)

If the State Chair determines the DEC has failed to hold a meeting with quorum for six consecutive months, the State Chair may direct Congressional District Chair(s) to convene a reorganization meeting with at least 14 days' notice.

Quorum

25% of membership must be present (excluding automatic members). The chair should confirm quorum with the secretary before calling the meeting to order.

Sample Meeting Agenda

  1. Call to Order
  2. Pledge of Allegiance
  3. Roll Call / Declaration of Quorum
  4. Approval of Agenda
  5. Approval of Minutes
  6. Officers Report (Chair, Vice Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, and/or State Committee)
  7. Old Business
  8. New Business
  9. Announcements
  10. Adjournment
💡 Guest speakers, local candidates, and celebrating volunteer effort are excellent additions. Use your meeting to celebrate and motivate — people support that which they help to create.

Voting & Proxies

Voting

Each member gets one (1) vote. Per FDP Charter Article I, Section 4.1.1, no vote shall be taken by secret ballot.

Proxies

Members unable to attend may submit a written proxy to a DEC officer before the meeting. No person may hold more than one (1) proxy. A proxy form is in the Appendix.

Steering Committee

Supervises and carries out business between DEC meetings. Actions must be ratified by the DEC at the next meeting. Consists of DEC officers and must include at least two State committee persons of different genders.

County Clubs

A Democratic club's charter falls within the jurisdiction of the county DEC where the majority of membership resides. A county DEC shall not establish a maximum number of clubs.

County Caucuses

Local caucus chapters are chartered by State Caucuses. State Caucuses are under the jurisdiction of the FDP. Contact the appropriate State Caucus for more information.

DCCA

The Democratic County Chairs Association promotes communication among DEC Chairs and Vice Chairs. Helps county parties improve governance, grow membership, and achieve electoral success.

Small County Coalition (SCC)

For DECs in counties with fewer than 40,000 registered Democrats. Monthly virtual meetings providing training and support. Represents nearly 500,000 registered Democrats collectively.

Compliance is more than a box to check — it is the foundation of a well-run County Party. Non-compliance risks include financial penalties, loss of trust, damaged relationships, and worst-case scenario, loss of Charter.

Record Keeping

All records (votes, financials, membership lists, bylaws, correspondence) should be kept for at least 3 years, preferably in cloud/digital storage.

Election ballots must be kept for a minimum of 6 months and are reviewable by a DEC member upon written request, supervised by two DEC officers.

Part 3: Election Year Compliance

Non-Endorsement Statement — Due: April 1

Signed statement sent to FDP and County SOE regarding the DEC's intention to endorse or not endorse candidates in a Primary. Required to qualify for Party assessment fees.

Citation: FLA STAT 103.121(4)(5)

Independent Expenditure Statement — Due: First Day of Qualifying

Notarized letter sent via certified mail to the Secretary of State affirming the DEC has not made and will not make an independent expenditure in support of or opposition to any candidate.

Citation: FLA. STAT. Sec. 106.087(1)(a)

Campaign Plan (Election Budget)

A DEC receiving Party assessment fees shall submit a campaign/election budget plan to the FDP and the Budget and Finance Committee for approval.
